Who I Am

Built From the Ground Up

My story didn't start in a gym — it started with a C2 vertebra fracture and a recovery that tested everything I had. When the doctors cleared me, I made a decision. I wasn't going back to where I was. I was going further.

Six years of unbroken commitment later, I've learned what real training looks like. Not the 30-minute shortcuts. Not the trendy programs. Real sessions — two hours or more — built around compound movements, heavy weights, and old-school fundamentals that have worked for decades.

My approach is simple: train hard, train natural, train smart. No pharmaceuticals. No gimmicks. Just the science of breaking muscle down and letting it rebuild stronger — and the discipline to keep showing up until it does.

I coach people the way I wish someone coached me — with encouragement, proper form, and a real understanding of how your specific body responds. Because what works for someone else may not work for you, and finding that out together is part of the process.

The Comeback

Three Days in a Coma.
Back Under the Bar.

I fell from a forklift and fractured my C2 vertebra — the bone right next to my carotid artery. The doctors said if it had broken all the way through, I would have bled out. No one would have known in time.

I was in a coma for weeks. When I finally woke up, I was in a hospital bed with tubes and a ventilator, my brother Joe at my side. I spent even more time in a neck brace, unable to do the one thing that kept me sane.

But even in recovery, I stayed disciplined. I ate clean. I did what I could. And the moment I was cleared, I was back under the bar. Because once this lifestyle gets in your blood, you don't quit. You adapt, you fight, and you come back stronger.

That experience didn't break me — it proved what I already knew: the gym isn't just about building muscle. It's about building the kind of person who refuses to stay down.
